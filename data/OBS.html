Winona County Oriental Bittersweet Project

The first job in which I was able to apply some of my GIS skills was with Winona County in southeastern Minnesota, under a grant provided by the Minnesota Department of Agriculture. Winona County has one of the Minnesota's most severe infestations of an aggressive woody vine, called Oriental Bittersweet. Oriental Bittersweet is a non-native, invasive species from Asia that has been sweeping across the United States. My job was to work with local landowners to diagnose their property, get coordinates and infestation intensity data, and aid them in a few hours of eradication. This particular map is the overview for the county, displaying parcels that have been evaluated, treated, or are pending some sort of action. Each property has its own map that shows a much more smaller scale view of the area mapped. The overview map, although it doesn't tell the viewer much about the infestation, was important for the Minnesota Department of Agriculture to see so that they could better understand the extent of Winona County's oriental bittersweet infestation.
