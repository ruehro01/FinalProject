About

My name is Ross Ruehmann, an aspiring cartographer, currently working on a Masters Degree from the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

My undergraduate studies were in Biology and Environmental Studies at Luther College in Decorah, IA. I took a couple of GIS courses late in my time at Luther. I found an interest in GIS and cartography and applied my skills through applied research projects in Ecology and GIS. Upon graduating, I spent several months with Winona County working with landowners to map and manage invasive species infestations within the county. My GIS career then took me into the U.S. Army as a Geospatial Engineer, where I use data and maps to aid commanders in the vizualization of the battlefield. I currently work for the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service as a GIS Pathways Student Trainee while I finish my degree from UW-Madison.

In my free time, I enjoy spending time outdoors traveling, camping, and hiking. I am an avid sports watcher and I spend much of my time exploring the country on my motorcycle in the warmer months.
